Coach's Picks

Books & gear I'd put in my own player's bag · Updated June 2026

This is not a ranked "best of the internet" list. It is the specific books and gear I have recommended to my own families, one a week in the newsletter, gathered in one place so you can find a past pick or browse them all at once. Baseball and softball, players and parents.

One rule: I only put something here if I would hand it to my own player. No filler, no chasing the most expensive thing on the rack.

Books for the Baseball & Softball Family

The Read · Mental Game
The Mental Game of Baseball
H.A. Dorfman

The book for the kid who carries the 0 for 4 home. Dorfman taught big leaguers that the at-bat is won before the pitch, and it reads just as true for a twelve year old. The missing language for separating the result from the work.

The Read · Development
The Talent Code
Daniel Coyle

How skill is actually built. Coyle digs into deep practice and why slow, focused, mistake-filled reps beat mindless volume. It changes how a parent thinks about a lesson, a cage session, and a bad day.

See it on Amazon →
The Read · The Long Game
Range
David Epstein

The case for the multi-sport athlete and for patience over early specialization. If you ever feel pressure to lock your kid into one sport at ten, read this first. It is the science behind playing the long game.

See it on Amazon →
The Read · Arm Care
The Arm
Jeff Passan

The book that explains why pitching arms break and why teenagers now fill the Tommy John waiting rooms. Read it once and you will never let anyone rush your player's arm again.

See it on Amazon →
The Read · For Parents
The Matheny Manifesto
Mike Matheny

A big-league manager's letter to parents on culture, expectations, and getting out of your kid's way. The book to read before the next travel-ball season, not after.
